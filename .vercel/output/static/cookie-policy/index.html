Cookie Policy

Last updated: May 5, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, which ones we use on n3global.tech, and how you can manage them. We try to keep our use of cookies to the minimum that's actually useful.

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that a website stores on your device (computer, tablet, phone) when you visit. They help the site remember things about your visit — such as preferences, login state, or anonymous traffic data. Similar technologies include local storage and session storage, which work in much the same way.

2. Cookies we use

We currently use the following minimal categories:

Essential cookies

Required for the website to work correctly. For example, ensuring that requests you make are routed to the right server. These cookies don't track you across other websites and don't carry advertising. They cannot be turned off without breaking the site.

Performance cookies (basic)

We may use first-party cookies set by our hosting provider (Vercel) to help us understand how the site is performing — for example, page load times and error rates. These don't identify individual visitors.

3. What we don't use

As of the last updated date above, we do not use:

  • Advertising or retargeting cookies
  • Behavioral profiling or cross-site tracking
  • Social-media tracking pixels (Facebook Pixel, etc.)
  • Third-party analytics that store personal identifiers

If we ever change this — for example, if we add Google Analytics or a chat widget — we will update this policy and, where required, ask for your consent before any non-essential cookies are set.

4. Third-party cookies from services we use

Some of the third-party services we rely on may set their own cookies when you interact with their content embedded on our site. Examples include:

  • Google Fonts — used to load typography. Google may log the request as part of normal traffic.
  • Vercel — our hosting provider, which may use cookies for security and infrastructure routing.

These services have their own privacy practices we do not control. Refer to their respective policies for full details.

5. How to manage cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers let you:

  • See which cookies are stored and delete them individually or in bulk
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies (note: this may break parts of many websites, including ours)
  • Use private/incognito mode to avoid storing cookies between sessions

Helpful guides for the major browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top will reflect any changes. We encourage you to review this page periodically.
